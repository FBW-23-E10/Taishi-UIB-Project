Houjicha

80g
€X.XX

Hojicha (roasted tea, hojicha) is a type of Japanese green tea, a drink made by roasting (hojicha) tea leaves. It is generally made by roasting sencha, bancha, or stem tea. Hojicha has a distinctive aroma, little bitterness or astringency, and is light on the palate. It is suitable for use during meals because it is mild and easy on the stomach. The custom of drinking hojicha varies considerably from region to region. Some hojicha is made from the finest and most expensive leaves, but it is generally ranked lower than gyokuro and sencha, and is considered to be on the same level as bancha and genmaicha, and is not considered to be a high-end Japanese tea. However, in Kyoto, where the custom of drinking hojicha is deeply rooted, it is no longer unusual for high-quality hojicha to be served at formal occasions in ryotei restaurants. In addition, hojicha has long been served at hospital meals and to replenish fluids during illness, but care should be taken because it contains the same amount of caffeine as sencha.
