Matcha (powdered green tea) is a finely ground powder of green tea leaves. In Japan, it is widely used as an ingredient in Japanese and Western confectionery and cuisine, as well as for drinking in the tea ceremony.Tencha is made by steaming chanoki leaves (tea leaves) and then drying them before grinding them in a tea mill. In addition to being used as a drink for Koicha (thick tea) and Usucha (thin tea), its refreshing bitterness is also favored for flavoring Japanese and Western confections, such as matcha-flavored ice cream, which has become a staple in Japan. Products in airtight containers such as plastic film bags or metal containers distributed for home use should be stored in a cool, dark place after opening to avoid deterioration.
