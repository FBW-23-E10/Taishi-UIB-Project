Genmaicha

80g
€X.XX

Genmaicha is a mixture of equal parts of green tea and roasted rice. It is a type of green tea or Japanese tea. The tea leaves are heated over high heat, and the rice is steamed and then roasted until it turns golden brown or popcorn-like. As a Japanese tea, it is positioned on the same level as bancha and hojicha. It is characterized by its light, fragrant aroma and taste. The quality of genmaicha depends more on the quality of the rice than on the tea leaves. The fragrance comes from the roasted rice, which is foxy in color, rather than from the rice that has been blasted. Therefore, the latter is considered to be the better quality for Genmaicha. Bancha and sencha are used for tea leaves, and glutinous rice is often used for rice. Bancha and sencha are the most common tea leaves, but hojigenmaicha, which is hojicha mixed with brown rice, also exists.
