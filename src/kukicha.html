Kukicha

kukicha is a type of green tea. It is also one of the most commonly consumed teas in Japan. In the narrow sense of the term, kukicha is a green tea grown without sunlight and made from new sprouts, which are dried by rubbing the tea leaves in several stages. In a broader sense, "kukicha" refers to matcha (powdered green tea), which is dried and powdered without rubbing, and "senjicha," in which the tea leaves are soaked in hot water (boiled) to extract the ingredients. Gyokuro and kabusecha differ from kukicha in the narrow sense in that they are grown in a way that blocks sunlight, and bancha uses large leaves and stems.
